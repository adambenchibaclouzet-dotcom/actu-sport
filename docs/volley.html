PARIS - The French men's volleyball team won a historic victory against Italy this Saturday december 13 evening at the "Paris Expo Porte de Versailles" in Paris. In an electric atmosphere in front of 15,000 spectators, the Blues won 3 sets to 2 (25-23, 22-25, 25-21, 23-25, 15-13).

After winning the first set in difficulty (25-23), the French saw the Italians equalize by dominating the second set (22-25). The third set saw France regain the lead thanks to an exceptional defense and decisive blocks from Benjamin Diez (25-21). But Italy, led by their star player Alessandro Michirains, forced the tie-break by winning 25-23 in the fourth set.

"It was an incredible match. We suffered, but we never gave up. The audience carried us to the end. It is an extraordinary collective victory." - Benjamin Toniutti, captain of the French team

The tie-break kept all its promises. Point by point, the two teams fought neck and neck in a rarely seen intensity. At 13-13, the tension was at its peak. It was finally Jean Patry who scored the last two decisive points, allowing France to win 15-13.

This capital victory allows France to take the lead in the European ranking of Olympic qualifications with 15 points, now ahead of Italy (14 points) and Poland (13 points). There are only two decisive matches left: against Poland on January 15 in Warsaw, then against Germany on January 20 in Berlin.

Head coach Andrea Giani said he was "extremely proud of his players" and praised "an exceptional collective performance". The Blues demonstrated that they had the mental and technical resources to compete with the best European nations. The road to the Olympic Games is still long, but this success against the champions of Europe gives all the hopes back to the French supporters.

The next meeting will be decisive. France will have to confirm against a Polish team also in the running for the qualification for the Volleyball Champions League. A match not to be missed under any circumstances for all volleyball enthusiasts.
